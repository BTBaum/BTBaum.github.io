Colon Hydrotherapy From Ancient to Modern Cleansing the bowel is an ancient therapy, dating back some 5,000 years, where the process of both enemas and laxative use were described in the oldest known medical texts of the Hindu Vedas. Dating back 3,000 years ago, the “Ebers Papyrus” was an Egyptian document which recorded medical practices dealing with bowel conditions and the practice of colon levage and laxative herbs used to clean the colon. Hippocrates, the famous Greek Doctor and the “Father of Medicine” in 400 B.C. left many writings behind referring to the various inventive ways of cleaning the intestines. In the 17th century enemas became very popular. Things went to extremes as many people had two to three enemas a day, fluids with colors and fragrances were all the rage. Many famous French kings, as Louis XIII and Louis XIV were ardent supporters and joined in the enema popularity. In the mid 1800’s, further advancements in technology inspired the development of “Colonic Machines”. While the mechanics may have changed, the overall method did not. Large amounts of water, contained in various elevated containers, were introduced in the bowel thru now rubber tubes. In the 1900’s, Dr. John Harvey Kellogg, (of Kellogg’s Corn Flakes) ran a “sanitarium” (now could be considered a health retreat or destination spa) where he treated thousands of people for a variety of ailments. He reported in the 1917 Journal of American Medicine, that in over 40,000 cases as a result of diet, exercise and enemas, in all but twenty cases, he had used no surgery for the treatment of gastrointestinal disease in his patients. Dr. Henry A. Cotton, in the 1930’s, published several articles in the Journal of American Medicine that discussed the use of colon cleansing and its successful outcomes. Colonics were widely popular but many of the doctors, who were strong proponents of colonics, also expressed basically the same concerns. Enemas or colonics will not cleanse the entire length of the bowel. Large amounts of water will disturb the “friendly” flora, as well as the electrolyte balance of the gastrointestinal tract or colon. The bowel could become as dependant on colonics as it would on laxatives. During the late 1970’s and early 1980’s, colon cleansing devices introduced technology that drastically changed the modality. Instrumentation now included water pressure and temperature monitoring and control, where the water did not just run in an out but had a chance to linger in the colon for the purpose of hydration. This brought about four significant developments: A slow gentle administration of filtered water into the bowel was effective and able to cleanse the entire length of the large intestine all the way to the cecum. Because low amounts of water were being introduced, balances within the bowel were much less likely to be disrupted. Water was now able to gently enter the bowel and “seep” into the “nooks and crannies” allowing the water to soften and loosen hardened feces. By changing the water temperature during the course of the session, they were now able to stimulate the muscles of the bowel itself, thus creating a toning effect, which eliminated the possibility of dependency on colonics. Equally significant was the invention of the dual hose delivery system and uniquely designed speculum. The administration of water now includes a small speculum, a water line and a waste line. Sanitization and cross contamination issues were virtually eliminated. So, with this new breed of instrumentation was the birth of modern “Professional Colon Hydro-therapy” and with it, a plethora of benefits are being recognized by people all over the globe.
